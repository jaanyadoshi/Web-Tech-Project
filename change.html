How can we help?

Watch GamesOne of the easiest ways to support women’s soccer is to watch. That’s right, simply clicking on your TV and tuning in helps the sport grow. Watching shows media channels that there is a demand for the sport. It is an incredibly simple way to show your support and move the needle for change. Of course, tune in to the big games, like the World Cup and NWSL final. But take it a step further. Watch other games. Pick a few teams to follow and root for. Be a consistent supporter.
Attend GamesOne of our favorite women’s soccer ideas for growing the sport is to get out there and watch in person! If you have a local professional team, then definitely go to at least one home game. You can also head to a local college game at any level. As a professional player, I can tell you that this kind of support goes a long way. It’s encouraging and empowering to see people in the stands and hear the crowd ebb and flow with the game. This is a great way to support players, and it also supports the sport. Again, it shows interest and contributes financially (however small).
Interact on Social MediaEngaging with women’s soccer content on social media is another way to learn about players and support the sport. Sharing and reposting is a great way to spread the word and get others on board. Here are some easy social media women’s soccer ideas:
  1. Like and comment on posts from or about women’s soccer.

  2. Follow the women’s soccer leagues and players.

  3. Vote in polls or respond to questions on social media.

  4. Share relevant articles or respost content on your page.

  5. Post footage from the games you attend.
Buy MerchPurchasing women’s soccer gear is a simple way to rep the sport and spread interest. But, it’s also a great way to directly support the teams, leagues, and players that you love.
Be A Voice – AdvocateBe an advocate for women’s soccer. There must be a widespread change in the way society views the sport. Silence will not get us there. When you hear someone bashing women’s soccer or players in an inappropriate way, help set the record straight. Don’t be afraid to let them know where you stand and why.
Stop Comparing It to Men’s SoccerThe constant comparison to men’s soccer is one of the most detrimental things to women’s soccer. Even with good intentions, the comparisons are harmful to the sport and female athletes. Instead of discussing the physical differences between genders, or which game is “harder” focus on appreciating the sport and athletes.
Get Involved with Girls Youth SoccerWhether you have a young female athlete in the family or not, there are plenty of ways to contribute to girls’ soccer. The push for women’s soccer starts with girls’ soccer.While a lot of girls play soccer when they are little, far fewer stay with the sport into college and beyond. Ideas for supporting girls’ youth soccer include:
  1. Volunteer with a local club or team.

  2. Coach a girls’ soccer team.

  3. Donate to a local girls’ soccer program.

  4. Sponsor jerseys and equipment.
